To test both the Foreground Screen Alerts (while the app is open) and the Background/Killed Output, I've set up two testing mechanisms for you:

1. Test the UI, Audio, & Vibration (Foreground)
I have just added a "Test Incoming Order Alarm" button right on the Profile (Settings) tab inside your app.

Open the App in your Expo Go / Emulator.
Navigate to the Settings tab.
Click the blue Test Incoming Order Alarm button.
The alarm should immediately go off, vibrate the device, and block the screen with the Accept/Reject full-screen modal!
(Hitting Accept or Reject will immediately kill the vibration and looping audio).

2. Test the Native Push Notifications (Background & Killed States)
To test how the system reacts when the phone is locked or the app is completely closed:

In your Node/Backend console, you'll see a log line that looks like FCM/Device Push Token: ExponentPushToken[xxxxxxx] when the app starts up. Copy this token.
Go to the official Expo Push Tool: https://expo.dev/notifications
Paste your token into the box.
In the Message Data (JSON) box at the bottom, paste this exact payload:
json
{
  "orderId": "mock_test_123"
}
Lock your phone or close the app entirely.
Hit Send Notification on the web tool.
Within 1 second, your locked phone should receive a MAX priority alert that forces a loud notification popup, bypassing normal silent configurations!
